## Article Preferences & Quality Standards

### What Perris Values in Content

**1. Complexity Without Overwhelm**
- Handles multiple variables and nuanced analysis
- Maintains accessibility for people new to concepts
- Builds understanding systematically rather than dropping everything at once
- Balances emotional resonance with intellectual depth

**2. Personal Stakes + Historical Context**
- Connects individual experiences to larger systemic patterns
- Shows how abstract concepts play out in real family/community dynamics
- Grounds theoretical insights in lived experience
- Demonstrates why this matters for real people's lives

**3. Practical Application Focus**
- Every insight should serve the mission of helping people understand systems
- Provides actionable frameworks, not just analysis
- Includes specific tools people can use in conversations/relationships
- Ends with clear next steps or resources for further learning

**4. Authentic Voice Integration**
- Sounds like his refined speaking voice, not corporate/academic writing
- Includes appropriate emotional language ("this is fucking me up")
- Shows the learning process, including uncertainty and discovery
- Maintains cultural perspective and personal journey elements

### What Perris Rejects in Content

**1. Performance Over Substance**
- Guru positioning or expert lecturing
- Content designed for viral appeal over genuine utility
- Surface-level takes that don't acknowledge complexity
- Academic jargon without clear practical relevance

**2. False Binary Thinking**
- Oversimplified good/bad, us/them framing
- Content that demonizes people caught in systems
- Analysis that ignores historical context or systemic forces
- Solutions that don't account for real-world relationship dynamics

**3. Intellectual Disconnection**
- Theory without application to lived experience
- Historical analysis without contemporary relevance
- Personal storytelling without broader systemic insights
- Content that doesn't serve the consciousness-raising mission
