# Perris Voice Analysis #6 - Forbidden Language & AI Writing Markers
*Logged 2026-02-26*

## Source Content
This comes from Perris's "Remove annoying words" document - a comprehensive list of disallowed words, phrases, and punctuation that create generic AI-sounding content.

## Core Philosophy
**Be very cautious about using metaphors and purple prose.** As an AI, I have a tendency to come up with metaphors and analogies that no human would say. It's better to avoid them.

## Disallowed Punctuation
- **Em-dashes "—"** - Completely forbidden (lose 100 points each)
- Use alternatives: comma, ellipsis, colon, or rewrite the passage
- Usually a comma works just as well
- **Exclamation points "!"** - Avoid entirely

## Disallowed Words & Phrases

### Generic Fluff Terms
- fluff
- here's the thing
- here's the kicker
- through the noise
- puzzle
- lightning
- magic
- double-edged sword
- fast track
- strike gold
- art/science analogy ("Storytelling isn't just an art. It's a science.")
- savvy
- transformative
- buzzword
- game-changer
- in a world of

### Overused Power Words
- superpower(s)
- superstar(s)
- secret weapon, weapons, arsenal
- harness
- thrive
- skyrocket
- soar ("watch your engagement soar")
- unlock
- unleash
- elevated
- evolve

### Corporate/Marketing Speak
- authentic
- vanity metrics
- pitch-slapped
- integrity
- forget ("forget vanity metrics")

### Cliché Patterns
- crickets ("launch to crickets" or other lack of engagement)
- void (e.g. shouting into the void)
- I used to… then I…
- unleashed the power of
- thank you for it ("your audience will thank you for it")
- It not about {thing}… it's about {something different}
- your truth ("share your truth")

### Overly Ethical Language
- ethical (or any other statements that are overly concerned with ethics)

### Action Verbs to Avoid
- delve
- elevate
- unleash
- unlock
- harness
- evolve

## Why These Words Are Forbidden

### They Sound Like Generic AI Content
These words and phrases are overused by AI writing tools and create content that feels:
- Inauthentic and robotic
- Like every other piece of AI-generated content
- Lacking in genuine human voice and personality
- Corporate and salesy rather than helpful

### They Don't Match Perris's Voice
Based on the previous voice analyses, Perris's authentic language patterns are:
- **Specific and precise** rather than fluffy
- **Conversational and direct** rather than corporate
- **Humble and curious** rather than claiming superpowers
- **Personal and grounded** rather than abstract and metaphorical

## Alternative Language Approaches

### Instead of Em-Dashes
- Use commas for brief pauses
- Use colons to introduce explanations
- Use ellipses for longer pauses
- Rewrite sentences to flow naturally without interruption

### Instead of Generic Power Words
- Use specific, concrete language
- Describe actual results and outcomes
- Use Perris's authentic expressions like "I noticed..." or "Here's what I'm seeing..."
- Ground everything in personal experience and specific examples

### Instead of Corporate Speak
- Use conversational, human language
- Be direct and specific
- Use Perris's natural phrases and expressions
- Focus on practical utility rather than hype

## Quality Control Checkpoint

Before publishing any content, scan for:
- [ ] No em-dashes (—)
- [ ] No forbidden words from the master list
- [ ] No generic metaphors or analogies that sound AI-generated
- [ ] Language sounds specifically like Perris, not generic AI content
- [ ] Conversational and direct rather than corporate or fluffy
- [ ] Grounded in specifics rather than abstract concepts

This forbidden language list is crucial for maintaining Perris's authentic voice because:

2. **Preserves conversational tone** that matches his natural speaking patterns
3. **Maintains intellectual credibility** by avoiding overused buzzwords
4. **Supports mission focus** by eliminating corporate-speak that doesn't serve the work
5. **Enhances readability** by using clear, direct language instead of fluffy filler

When writing in Perris's voice, I will:
1. **Scan every sentence** for forbidden words and punctuation
2. **Rewrite immediately** if any appear
3. **Use specific alternatives** that match his natural language patterns
4. **Prioritize clarity and directness** over stylistic flourishes
5. **Ground everything** in concrete examples and personal experience

This creates content that truly sounds like Perris wrote it, rather than AI trying to imitate human writing.
