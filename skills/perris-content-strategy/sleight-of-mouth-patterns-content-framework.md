# Sleight of Mouth Patterns - Content Framework for Both Brands
*Logged 2026-02-26*

## Source Context
14 Sleight of Mouth patterns from NLP that can be repurposed for content creation, scripts, blogs, and marketing copy for both Perris's personal brand (consciousness-raising) and CyphrCam business (dance community).

## Overview
Sleight of Mouth patterns are linguistic tools for reframing beliefs and perspectives. They're particularly powerful for Perris's mission because they help people see their perceived limitations as strengths, and shift from limitation-based thinking to possibility-based thinking.

## The 14 Sleight of Mouth Patterns

### 1. Intention
**Focus on the positive purpose behind a belief or statement**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "My scattered thinking holds me back"
- **Reframe:** "Your mind explores multiple dimensions simultaneously because it's designed to recognize patterns and connections that others miss. That's actually a superpower for creativity and innovation."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I need expensive equipment to create good dance videos"
- **Reframe:** "Your desire for quality shows you care about representing your artistry authentically. CyphrCam was designed because dancers like you deserve tools that match your creative standards."

### 2. Redefining
**Substitute words with similar but different implications**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I'm too unfocused"
- **Reframe:** "You're not unfocused - you're multi-dimensional. You have the ability to hold multiple perspectives simultaneously, which is exactly what the world needs from creative entrepreneurs."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I'm being disruptive when I practice"
- **Reframe:** "You're not being disruptive - you're being dedicated to your craft. CyphrCam lets you channel that dedication without compromise."

### 3. Consequence
**Highlight positive effects or outcomes**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I keep jumping between different projects"
- **Reframe:** "And because you explore multiple domains, you're able to create unique combinations and insights that single-focus people would never discover. Some of the most innovative businesses come from connecting seemingly unrelated fields."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I spend too much time editing videos"
- **Reframe:** "And that time you spend editing could be spent practicing and improving your craft. CyphrCam gives you back those hours to focus on what matters - your artistry."

### 4. Chunk Down
**Break things into smaller components**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I'm bad at business"
- **Reframe:** "Which specific aspect of business? The creative development? The systems? The marketing? Because I've seen you come up with brilliant innovative ideas, so maybe it's just about strengthening certain skills rather than being 'bad at business' as a whole."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "Technology is too complicated for me"
- **Reframe:** "Which part feels complicated? The recording? The syncing? The sharing? Because CyphrCam handles the technical complexity - you just focus on dancing."

### 5. Chunk Up
**Move to a larger, more general category**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I can't stick to one niche"
- **Reframe:** "Is it really about niches, or is it about making an impact? Because some of the most successful creators and entrepreneurs transcend traditional niches by focusing on their unique perspective and value."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I need to choose between quality and convenience"
- **Reframe:** "Is it really about choosing, or is it about having tools that work as hard as you do? The best artists throughout history had tools that enhanced their creativity, not hindered it."

### 6. Analogy
**Use metaphors and comparisons**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "My mind is too chaotic"
- **Reframe:** "You know how a jazz musician improvises by weaving together different melodies? That's how your mind works - it's not chaos, it's creative improvisation. The key is learning to conduct your mental orchestra."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "Recording disrupts my flow state"
- **Reframe:** "Think of how professional athletes have their performance recorded without it affecting their game. CyphrCam works like an invisible observer - capturing your art without interfering with your flow."

### 7. Change Frame Size
**Shift the timeframe or scope of reference**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I'm wasting time exploring different interests"
- **Reframe:** "Looking back 5 years from now, would you regret not exploring these interests? Many successful entrepreneurs spent years experimenting before finding their unique combination of skills and passions."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I'm not ready to start creating content yet"
- **Reframe:** "In 5 years, would you rather look back at hundreds of hours of practice footage showing your journey, or wish you had started documenting your growth today?"

### 8. Another Outcome
**Shift focus to a different goal**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I need to focus on just one thing to be successful"
- **Reframe:** "Is the goal really to focus on one thing, or is it to create value and impact in your unique way? Some of the most successful creators integrate multiple passions into something entirely new."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I need to sound professional when I practice"
- **Reframe:** "Is the goal really to sound professional, or is it to connect authentically with your music? The most powerful dance videos come from that authentic connection, not perfect audio setup."

### 9. Model of the World
**Consider a different perspective or worldview**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "Normal people don't work this way"
- **Reframe:** "From the perspective of creative innovators throughout history, 'normal' ways of working would have felt incredibly limiting. Think about Leonardo da Vinci - he was an artist, scientist, inventor, and writer all at once."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "Real dancers don't need technology to practice"
- **Reframe:** "From the perspective of professional dancers who've used CyphrCam, technology becomes invisible when it truly serves the art. The tool disappears and only the expression remains."

### 10. Reality Strategy
**Examine how someone determines something is true**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I know I'll fail"
- **Reframe:** "How specifically do you predict future outcomes? Because I've noticed that when you follow your intuitive way of working, you actually create remarkable results."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I know this won't work for my style of dance"
- **Reframe:** "How do you usually know if something will work for your style? Have you tried practicing with the music directly in your ears while recording? Most dancers are surprised by how natural it feels."

### 11. Counter Example
**Find exceptions to the rule**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "You can't be successful without focusing on one thing"
- **Reframe:** "What about Richard Branson? Or Elon Musk? Or Marie Forleo? They've all built successful businesses across multiple industries by leveraging their diverse interests."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "You can't create professional content without expensive equipment"
- **Reframe:** "What about [specific dancer] who built their following using just their phone? Or [battle winner] whose breakthrough video was recorded at home? Some of the most viral dance content comes from authentic moments, not expensive setups."

### 12. Hierarchy of Criteria
**Appeal to higher-level values**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I should stick to a conventional career path"
- **Reframe:** "What's more important - following convention, or creating genuine value by expressing your authentic gifts?"

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "I should wait until I'm better before recording myself"
- **Reframe:** "What's more important - appearing perfect, or honoring your journey and growth as an artist? Your authentic practice is more valuable than polished performance."

### 13. Apply to Self
**Apply the belief to itself**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "All absolute statements are limiting"
- **Reframe:** "Isn't that itself an absolute statement? Maybe the key is finding which approaches work best in different contexts."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "Technology always complicates things"
- **Reframe:** "Isn't that statement itself a complication of how technology could work? What if some technology is designed to simplify rather than complicate?"

### 14. Meta Frame
**Create a belief about the belief**

**Personal Brand Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "My different interests are a liability"
- **Reframe:** "That belief comes from an industrial-age mindset where specialization was key. In today's creative economy, the ability to combine different domains is increasingly valuable."

**CyphrCam Example:**
- **Limiting Belief:** "Using technology to dance is not authentic"
- **Reframe:** "That belief assumes technology and authenticity are opposites. But what if technology that truly serves artistry actually enhances authenticity by removing barriers between you and your expression?"

### Content Creation Strategy

**Hook + Sleight of Mouth Pattern Formula:**
1. **Present common limiting belief** (hook)
2. **Apply sleight of mouth pattern** (reframe)
3. **Provide supporting evidence** (validation)
4. **Connect to larger mission** (integration)

**Example for Personal Brand:**
1. **Hook:** "Everyone says you need to pick one thing to be successful"
2. **Counter Example Pattern:** "But look at [successful polymath examples]"
3. **Evidence:** "Studies show interdisciplinary thinking drives innovation"
4. **Mission:** "Your diverse interests aren't holding you back - they're preparing you to solve problems others can't see"

**Example for CyphrCam:**
1. **Hook:** "Most dancers think you need expensive equipment for good videos"
2. **Consequence Pattern:** "But what if the best videos come from authentic moments, not expensive setups?"
3. **Evidence:** "Here's [dancer] whose viral video was shot with just their phone and CyphrCam"
4. **Mission:** "Your artistry matters more than your equipment"
