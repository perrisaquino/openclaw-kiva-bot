## How to Use This Skill

### Step 1: Determine Content Context
**What are you creating?**
- Sales page / Landing page
- Newsletter / Email sequence  
- Blog article / Long-form content
- Social media post / Video script
- Product launch copy
- Community-building content

**Which brand?**
- Personal Brand (consciousness-raising, creative polymaths)
- CyphrCam (dance community, freestyle culture)
- Cross-pollination content (both audiences)

### Step 2: Select Appropriate Voice Mode
**Current authentic state** (from `perris-voice-analysis-1-content-strategy.md`):
- **Maya State** - Sacred rage/emotional processing → deep decolonization content
- **Dr. Sarah State** - Intellectual integration → interdisciplinary frameworks  
- **David State** - Curious exploration → gentle awakening content
- **Roberto State** - Protective urgency → legacy wisdom content
- **Alex State** - Strategic integration → conscious business content
- **Mike Bridge State** - Frustrated but strategic → entry-point content for sleepers

### Step 3: Apply Core Frameworks

**Hook Strategy** (from `perris-voice-analysis-7-hook-framework.md`):
- Use SEED framework: Share → Explore → Engage → Discover
- Choose from 5 hook categories: Puzzle, Personal Stakes, Pattern Recognition, Reframe, Learning Journey
- Test against: "Would past self be curious?" "Does this make people feel smart?"

**Content Structure** (from `perris-voice-analysis-2-article-writing.md`):
- **Long-form:** Personal catalyst → Research deep-dive → Pattern recognition → Integration wisdom → Community invitation
- **Short-form:** Personal moment → Pattern demonstration → Practical application → Community question
- **Bullets:** Use Clayton Makepeace frameworks adapted for mission

**Voice Consistency** (from `perris-voice-analysis-6-forbidden-language.md`):
- Avoid em-dashes, exclamation points, and generic AI language
- No "superpower," "game-changer," "unlock," "transform" unless genuinely authentic
- Maintain conversational directness over corporate speak

### Step 4: Audience-Specific Adaptation

**Personal Brand Audience** (from `perris-personal-brand-audience-psychographics.md`):
- Address scattered thinking as pattern recognition advantage
- Speak to financial/creative soul conflict
- Validate complex thinking patterns
- Reference systems of oppression awareness

**CyphrCam Audience** (from `cyphrCam-target-avatar-jordan.md`):
- Address flow state protection and technical barriers
- Speak to neighbor/roommate relationship strain
- Use authentic freestyle dance community language
- Focus on artistry over equipment

### Step 5: Apply Persuasion Techniques

**Sleight of Mouth Patterns** (choose 1-2 per piece):
- Redefining: "You're not unfocused - you're multi-dimensional"
- Consequence: "And because you explore multiple domains, you create unique insights"
- Analogy: "Like a jazz musician improvising - that's creative orchestration, not chaos"
- Counter Example: "What about [successful polymath examples]?"

**Bullet Frameworks** (for sales copy):
- How To: "How to turn scattered thinking into systematic advantage"
- Secret To: "The secret to perfect audio-video sync that eliminates editing hours"
- Warning: "WARNING: Poor audio sync is destroying your battle scene reputation"
- Are You: "Are you hiding your best insights because they seem 'too complex'?"

## Content Quality Checklist

### Voice Authenticity
- [ ] Sounds specifically like Perris, not generic AI content
- [ ] Uses his natural language patterns and expressions
- [ ] Maintains intellectual humility ("I'm still figuring this out...")
- [ ] Shows learning process, not just final conclusions
- [ ] Includes appropriate cultural specificity (Filipino perspectives, dance community language)

### Mission Alignment
- [ ] Serves consciousness-raising or artistic authenticity goals
- [ ] Creates curiosity rather than defensiveness
- [ ] Builds bridges rather than creates division
- [ ] Provides practical frameworks people can actually use
- [ ] Maintains community-over-audience positioning

### Audience Resonance
- [ ] Addresses specific pain points from psychographic research
- [ ] Uses language patterns that resonate with target audience
- [ ] Creates "I feel seen" moments through accurate problem articulation
- [ ] Provides hope without overpromising
- [ ] Includes specific, credible examples and details

### Persuasion Effectiveness
- [ ] Creates curiosity loops that demand further investigation
- [ ] Reframes limitations as potential strengths
- [ ] Uses social proof and counter-examples appropriately
- [ ] Builds desire through benefit layering
- [ ] Ends with clear, authentic next steps
