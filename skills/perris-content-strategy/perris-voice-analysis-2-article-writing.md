# Perris Voice Analysis #2 - Long-Form Article Writing System
*Logged 2026-02-26*

## Source Content
This comes from Perris's comprehensive article writing system for his "Akino" content brand - a detailed breakdown of how he approaches long-form content (2,000-4,000 words) that serves consciousness-raising and collective liberation.

## Core Philosophy: "Walking With People"

### "Skin in the Game" Approach
- Never present purely theoretical concepts
- Every insight backed by lived experience
- Process through emotions, intellect, spirit, intuition
- Demonstrate vulnerability and real human struggle
- **"I'm not talking AT people, I'm walking WITH people"**

### Story Integration Protocol
**When to ask for stories:**
- Concept needs grounding in real experience
- Transformation moment needs illustration
- Resistance or struggle needs humanizing
- Stakes demonstration needed

**Story prompting questions:**
- "Can you think of a specific time when [concept] played out in your life?"
- "When did you first resist this idea? What changed your mind?"
- "How did learning this affect your relationships/work/decisions?"

## Authentic Voice DNA

### Natural Language Patterns

**Opening Rhythms:**
- "So I've been learning some things that are..."
- "This is gonna sound weird, but I keep thinking about..."
- "Can we talk about something that's been fucking me up?"
- "So I've been reading [book] and it's fucking me up because..."

**Connection-Making:**
- "And here's where it gets interesting..."
- "This connects to something I read about..."
- "The pattern I'm seeing is..."
- "When you read [Author A] alongside [Author B], something interesting happens..."

**Intellectual Humility:**
- "I'm still figuring this out, but..."
- "Help me understand if I'm missing something..."
- "I could be wrong about this, but..."
- "I'm literally figuring this out as I write, but here's what I'm seeing..."

**Community Building:**
- "I just wanted to share this..."
- "I don't know if other people see this too, but it's been on my mind"
- "What patterns are you seeing?"
- "I'm genuinely curious about your perspective on this"

### What Perris DOESN'T Say

**Avoid these patterns:**
- ❌ "Collective awakening" → ✅ "helping people understand how things actually work"
- ❌ "It's fucking with my worldview" → ✅ "It's fucking me up" or "It's changing everything"
- ❌ "And that's when it clicked for me" → ✅ "And that's when I realized..."
- ❌ "Let me walk you through what I discovered" → ✅ "This is what I found:"
- ❌ "I don't know if this will land" → ✅ "I don't know if other people see this too"
- ❌ "Entrepreneurial success" positioning → ✅ "As someone who's worked in [context]..."

## Content Structure Templates

### Long-Form Articles (3000+ words): "The Deep Synthesis"
**The Akino Arc: Personal → Pattern → Principle → Practice**

**I. The Catalyst (400 words)**
```
"So I've been diving deep into [specific topic] because [personal reason/emotional trigger]. 
At first I thought [initial assumption], but then I started reading [specific sources] 
and holy shit, [mind-blown moment]..."
```

**II. The Excavation (1500 words)**
```
"This is what I found: [Author/Source 1] talks about [concept], and when you combine 
that with what [Author/Source 2] says about [related concept], you start seeing this 
pattern where [synthesis insight]..."
- Use natural "and then" "and here's the thing" connectors
- Include specific quotes and page numbers
- Show real-time connection-making process
```

**III. The Integration (800 words)**
```
"Here's what this means for us: [practical framework]. As someone who's [your specific 
background/perspective], I see how this applies to [specific situation]..."
```

**IV. The Invitation (300 words)**
```
"I'm still figuring this stuff out, and I thought someone else might find it interesting too. 
If you want to dive deeper, here are the books/sources that got me thinking about this: 
[specific resources]. What patterns are you seeing?"
```

### Short-Form Essays (800-1200 words): "The Insight Download"
- **The Spark** (200 words) - Personal realization moment
- **The Connection** (400 words) - Pattern recognition and examples  
- **The Application** (300 words) - Practical implications
- **The Community Prompt** (100 words) - Genuine curiosity questions

### Micro Essays (300-600 words): "The Truth Bombs"
- **The Provocation** (100 words) - "Can we talk about [thing that's bothering you]?"
- **The Reality Check** (300 words) - "Here's what's actually happening..."
- **The Reframe** (100 words) - "So instead of [old frame], what if [new frame]?"

## Voice Guidelines by Platform

### Newsletter/Email
**"I thought this was cool" sharing voice:**
- Write like sharing something interesting with a friend
- "I've been reading this thing and thought you might find it interesting..."
- "I don't know if other people see this too, but it's been on my mind..."
- Ask questions from genuine curiosity, not for engagement metrics

### Articles/Blog  
**Refined but conversational analysis:**
- Clean up stream-of-consciousness but keep connection-making energy
- "As someone who's worked in [context]..." (not success positioning)
- Include learning process: "When I read X, I started seeing Y..."
- Balance intellectual rigor with "I think this is cool" accessibility
- End with genuine curiosity: "What patterns are you seeing?"

## The 5-Artifact Development Process

### Artifact 1: Idea Archaeology + Story Mining
- Map out all ideas, connections, insights shared
- Identify core thesis and supporting threads
- **Mine for relevant personal experiences**
- Recognize emotional undertones and energy patterns
- Note potential blind spots needing research

### Artifact 2: Audience Psychology Analysis  
- Match content to target avatar psychological profile
- Analyze their pain points, fears, desires
- Determine optimal language patterns
- Predict resistance points and how to address them

### Artifact 3: Research & Verification Matrix
- Ground insights in credible sources
- Research supporting academic studies, books, historical facts
- Find real case studies (no hypotheticals)
- Verify quotes and citations
- Cross-reference with Perris's reading influences

### Artifact 4: Structure & Flow Design
- Create SEO-friendly title maintaining authentic voice
- Design hook for target avatar attention
- Structure main sections for logical flow
- Integrate personal narrative with analytical insights
- Plan community-building elements

### Artifact 5: Draft Creation
- 2,000-4,000 word article in authentic voice
- Remove verbal fillers but keep connection-making energy
- Blend personal narrative with researched insights
- Use appropriate avatar language patterns
- Include verified sources and citations

## Quality Checkpoints

**Before Publishing:**
- [ ] All quotes and citations verified through web search
- [ ] Real case studies used (no hypotheticals)
- [ ] Claims backed by credible sources
- [ ] Tone matches target avatar's psychology
- [ ] Personal stakes and vulnerability shared

- [ ] Fellow seeker positioning (not expert/guru)
