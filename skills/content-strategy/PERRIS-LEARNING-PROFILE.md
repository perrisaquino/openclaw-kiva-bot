# Perris Aquino — Learning Profile & Portrait

_Last updated: March 3, 2026_

## How Perris Learns

### Core Learning Style: Resonance-Driven Synthesis

Perris doesn't learn through discipline or forced curriculum. He learns through **resonance** — when material mirrors his lived experience, he goes deep. His most annotated books are the ones where he kept writing "this is me" in the margins. When something doesn't resonate, he moves on without guilt. This isn't laziness. It's a highly tuned filter for signal vs. noise.

### The Learning Loop

1. **Encounter** — Find material through curiosity, not obligation (books, podcasts, articles, YouTube, conversations)
2. **Resonate** — The gut responds (Sacral Authority). Does this speak to my experience? Does it feel true?
3. **Extract** — Pull out the framework, mental model, or principle underneath the surface content
4. **Connect** — Cross-pollinate with other domains. How does this copywriting principle relate to decolonization? How does flow state in dance relate to Barbara Oakley's diffuse mode?
5. **Embody** — Process through movement. Dance on it, walk on it, let the body integrate what the mind captured
6. **Apply** — Build something, share something, create something that concentrates the knowledge into usefulness. But also: apply it into lived experience. Not every application is a deliverable. Sometimes it's how you show up in a moment differently because of something you read three years ago. How you navigate a relationship, process a breakup, move through a space, make a decision. The knowledge becomes part of how you live.
7. **Reflect** — Voice memos, brain dumps, annotations. Close the loop by articulating what was learned

### Critical Belief: Learning Must Serve Application (Into Lived Experience)

**Learning for learning's sake is not the full expression of the practice.** Perris believes that real learning is completed through application, through doing something with what you've learned. Building. Sharing. Creating something that concentrates knowledge into something useful for the world. But application isn't limited to deliverables and products. It also means applying knowledge into lived experience, into how you actually move through life. The loop isn't: read → understand → done. The loop is: read → understand → BUILD/LIVE → share → and through that building, living, and sharing, deepen the understanding further. Knowledge that stays in the vault is unfinished knowledge.

This is why CyphrCam matters as a learning project too: it's the application layer for everything he's learned across marketing, coding, dance, storytelling, business, and design. It's knowledge made real.

### Framework Collector

Perris hunts for the skeleton underneath information. Charlie Munger's "latticework of mental models" is his north star. He doesn't want isolated facts. He wants operating systems, decision-making structures, and principles that transfer across domains. His goal (in his own words): "to be unconsciously competent in my decision making, by building up the latticework of these mental models."

### Cross-Pollinator

His reading spans copywriting (Breakthrough Advertising, Cashvertising), decolonization (Pedagogy of the Oppressed, Brown Skin White Minds, The Jakarta Method), esoteric knowledge (Secret Teachings of All Ages, Nag Hammadi, BioGeometry), business (80/20 Sales, E-Myth, Profit First), psychology (Alter Ego Effect, Great Mental Models, Wanting), and movement/consciousness. No lane. Everything connects.

### Thinks in Frameworks, Feels in Stories

Loves structure: CARPULS, DRY Principle, Idea Compass, Framework Thinking. But what actually sticks is narrative. The Antaeus myth. Anthony choking on the basketball court. Tony Hsieh in a dirty Mazda. Stories encode frameworks in memory. Without the story, it's just theory.

### Movement IS Thinking

Dance and movement aren't exercise. They are his diffuse-mode processor. He explicitly maps Barbara Oakley's focused/diffuse model to his own practice: focused mode for coding and research, then dance/movement to let the subconscious integrate. This is flow state at theta frequency. Movement is how breakthroughs happen.

### Progressive Summarization Practitioner

Uses Tiago Forte's progressive summarization with attention hierarchy:
1. Regular text → base content
2. *Italics* → organic emphasis
3. **Bold** → stands out
4. Underline → another layer
5. **Bold + Highlight** → highest importance (content gold)

Multiple passes on important material. The formatting layers reveal what caught his attention most deeply.

### The Hidden Classrooms Philosophy

Perris's most developed original learning concept. Education is everywhere, not in institutions. YouTube channels, Twitter accounts, books in sequence, curated info hubs, conversations. The framework: self-synchronize first → ask better questions → leverage AI → design "reality engines" around serendipity.

### Self-Taught Polymath

258 books, 873 articles, podcasts spanning BioGeometry to copywriting to sacred geometry to Filipino psychology to mental models to fascism. No degree required. Reverse-engineered Agora's billion-dollar copywriting frameworks alongside reading Pedagogy of the Oppressed. No curriculum would combine these. His brain does.

### Learning Gap (Self-Aware)

Captures brilliantly. Processing pipeline has drop-off. Zettelkasten permanent notes folder is thin (8 files vs. 30+ fleeting notes). The raw material is gold. The refinement step from fleeting → literature → permanent → APPLICATION could be tighter. The Quick Capture system helps bridge this.

## Who Perris Is — The Full Portrait

### The Synthesizer

His brain works in webs, not lanes. He reads Brown Skin White Minds and connects it to fascism, which connects to exit planning, which connects to CyphrCam revenue, which connects to the 12-year-old who found movement as medicine. Every book talks to every other book in his vault.

### The Decolonizer (Of His Own Mind)

The deepest thread in everything. Systematically unpacking layers of conditioning: colonialism, Iglesia ni Cristo, entrepreneur hustle culture, the "struggling artist" identity. Deciding piece by piece what's actually his and what was installed. "Belief systems = BS" isn't a joke. It's methodology.

### The Hermit-Opportunist (2/4 Profile)

Needs deep cave time for mastery (the reading, the solo coding sessions, the vault-building). But thrives through network and connection. The Hidden Classrooms concept bridges both: learning in solitude through curated connections to other minds.

### What He's Actually Building

CyphrCam isn't a startup pitch. It's:

- The merger of every skill and identity he's ever had (no more mask)
- An application layer for everything he's learned
- A tool so dancers never have to choose between being present and capturing the moment
- Income to fund leaving the US and fixing his health

Not money. Not status. The ability to dance without worrying about vision loss. To leave a country that doesn't feel safe. To create without performing. To be one person, not two. To build something that helps other movers access the thing movement gave him.
