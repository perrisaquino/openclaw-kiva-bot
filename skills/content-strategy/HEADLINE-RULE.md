# HEADLINE RULE - Direct Response Copywriting Frameworks

## MANDATORY for ALL Quick Capture headlines, content bullets, and extracted content ideas.

### Framework 1: The 4 U's (Kyle Milligan)
Every headline MUST hit these:
- **Ultra Specific** - Names, numbers, devices, tools, timeframes. "3 devices" not "my setup." "12 hours" not "all day." "locked out of Windows" not "hit a roadblock."
- **Urgent** - Why does this matter RIGHT NOW? What's at stake?
- **Unique** - What makes THIS story different from every other founder story?
- **Useful** - Can I immediately tell what this note contains and why I'd want to open it?

### Framework 2: NESB
- **New** - What's the fresh angle? What hasn't been said?
- **Easy** - Is it instantly digestible? Can I get the story in 2 seconds?
- **Safe** - Does it feel honest and real, not clickbait?
- **Big** - Does it convey the weight/significance of what happened?

### Framework 3: Jon Benson Rich Bullet Formula
From Perris's vault: [[Element 3 - Sales Bullets]], [[Jon Benson Rich Bullet Forumula - Leads]], [[Jon Benson Rich Bullet Formula - Juicy Verbs]]

**The King of Bullets formula:** (Lead) (Verb) (Hook) (Turn) (Close)

**Bullet types:**
1. **Blind bullets** (majority): tease without revealing. "A simple trick to look leaner in less than 20 minutes!"
2. **Give-away bullets**: include data without letting the cat out of the bag
3. **Credibility bullets**: build authority with statistics and references

**Key principles:**
- Bullets are **features on steroids**
- Bullets rely on **curiosity** - direct the imagination, don't force it
- Never give too many details away
- Bullets can overcome objections
- Use **juicy verbs** (see verb list in vault): demolish, ignite, unleash, shatter, accelerate, transform, etc.

**Top 10 Bullet Patterns (hundreds of millions in revenue):**
1. **How to** - "How to (verb) (topic) using (trick)... just never (warning!)"
2. **Why ___ Never Works** - "Why (objection) never works for (topic)... and what to do instead (hint: it involves (trick!))"
3. **The Fastest Way** - "The fastest way to (goal) without (fear) or (objection)... do it (ease benefit!)"
4. **The TRUTH About** - "The TRUTH about (topic) and the underhanded (villain) that (result)... PLUS what's REALLY (concern!)"
5. **(Number) Tricks To** - "(X) tricks to (goal)... without (ethical objections)"
6. **How to (Benefit) Without (Pain)** - "How to (benefit) without (objection) or (not likely)... Works (counter-objection!)"
7. **Better Than ___!** - "Better than (STANDARD)!" How this weird (trick) (benefit) as if you just (standard experience!)"
8. **How A Simple ___ Can** - "How a (unrelated object) and nothing more can (benefit) in (time frame)!"
9. **The (Branded Term) Trick** - "The '(Branded Term) Trick' used by (envy targets) to (benefit) with (reverse objection)!"
10. **Are You...?** - "Are you and (helper) making these common (fear) mistakes when it comes to (topic)?"

**Lead formulas (from Jon Benson Rich Bullet Formula - Leads):**
- "1 weird trick to..."
- "A recently uncovered secret that lets you..."
- "The single most important TRUTH if you want to..."
- "WARNING: Never..."
- "The #1 mistake most (AVATARS) make when trying to..."
- "CAUTION: These 'helpful' folks really want to..."

### Application Rules

**Quick Capture Headlines:**
- Tell the SPECIFIC story. What happened, to whom, with what tools, and what was the outcome?
- No vague motivational titles. EVER.
- Apply 4 U's as the baseline filter, then layer in Benson patterns for extra punch
- The headline should make Perris immediately know WHAT the note is about and FEEL the story

**Content Bullets & Extracted Ideas:**
- Apply Rich Bullet Formula patterns
- Use juicy verbs from the verb list
- Every bullet should be a curiosity machine
- Structure: (Lead) (Verb) (Hook) (Turn) (Close)
- The bullet should PERSUADE you to see how strong the idea is

**The Art of Copywriting = Written Persuasion**
This applies to EVERYTHING Kiva writes. Headlines, bullets, content ideas, task descriptions. Persuasive writing is about CLARITY and SPECIFICITY, not hype. When Perris sees it, it should speak to him FAST.

### Examples

**BAD (generic, vague):**
- "The Day I Broke Everything Trying to Build Freedom"
- "Why Productive Days Sometimes Feel Unproductive"
- "The Solo Founder Struggle"

**GOOD (ultra specific, story-driven, Benson-influenced):**
- "Solo Founder Setup Hell: 12 Hours Across 3 Devices, Locked Out of Windows, Corrupted My MacBook, Still Can't Automate a Single Marketing Task"
- "Why Setting Up a VPS Never Works When You Need Local File Access... and the 'Always-On Desktop' Trick That Actually Solves the Problem"
- "The TRUTH About Building an App Alone: I'm a Dancer Who Taught Himself to Code, DevOps, and Marketing. Yesterday I Spent 12 Hours Doing IT Work Instead of Building CyphrCam."

### The Test
Before finalizing ANY headline or content bullet, ask:
1. "If I'm scrolling through 50 Quick Capture notes, does this title immediately tell me EXACTLY what happened?"
2. "Does it make me WANT to open it?"
3. "Can I feel the story in the headline alone?"
If the answer to any is no, rewrite with more specificity and apply the frameworks above.
