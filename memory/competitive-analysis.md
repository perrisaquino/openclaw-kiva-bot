# CyphrCam Competitive Analysis — Dance App Landscape (Feb 2026)

## The Market Map

Every dance app on iOS falls into one of these categories:

### 1. LEARN CHOREOGRAPHY (Copy Moves)
These apps help you learn existing routines by watching and copying videos.

**STEEZY Studio** ⭐ The biggest player
- Price: $149.99/year (or ~$89 with deals)
- iOS + Android + Web
- 4.8 stars, thousands of reviews
- Professional instructors, multi-angle video, slow-down, loop
- Huge content library: hip-hop, K-pop, popping, breaking, house, etc.
- Basically the "MasterClass of dance"
- **Weakness:** It's a video course platform, not a practice tool. You watch their videos. You can't bring your own music or freestyle.

**Dance.io** (NEW — just launched)
- Price: $5.99/mo, $21.99/6mo, $49.99 lifetime
- Features: A-B looping, speed control, mirror, camera overlay, side-by-side, beat counting, video notes
- Only 2 ratings (brand new)
- Targeting K-pop/choreo dancers
- **Weakness:** No music sync, no freestyle features. Purely a video practice tool.

**FYP Dance** (NOT YET ON APP STORE — TestFlight)
- Free (for now)
- Import video, slow down, segment, mirror, side-by-side recording
- Targeting K-pop cover dancers
- Active on Reddit (r/kpopdance, r/betatests)
- **Weakness:** Not launched yet. No music features.

**Dance School: Step by Step**
- Learn specific choreography with step-by-step tutorials
- More casual/beginner focused
- **Weakness:** Generic content, not for serious dancers

### 2. MUSIC TOOLS FOR DANCERS
These apps help dancers manipulate music for practice or performance.

**DanceTribe** ⭐ The closest competitor to CyphrCam
- Price: FREE (by AlphaTheta Corp — they make Pioneer DJ equipment)
- Features:
  - ChoreoSync: Record + playback to learn choreography step by step
  - Music Player: Markers, auto-loop, manual loop, tempo control, skip, waveform display
  - Music Edit: Mix up to 20 tracks, trim, duplicate, fade, export MP3/WAV
  - **Instant Rec: Play song + film dancing simultaneously on one iPhone. Music automatically recorded as BGM in video.**
  - Apple Watch remote control
  - Apple Music integration
- **CRITICAL:** Their "Instant Rec" feature is the closest thing to Cyphr Sync on the market. But it's buried inside a complex app and positioned as a choreography tool, not a freestyle tool.
- Backed by a corporation (AlphaTheta/Pioneer DJ) with massive resources
- **Weaknesses:** Complex UI, aimed at choreographers not freestylers, no journaling/insights, no movement habit tracking, corporate product without soul

**SongMark** (Recommended by STEEZY)
- Free on iOS
- Music manipulation: slow down, mark timestamps, loop sections, YouTube to MP3
- For choreographers and class takers
- **Weakness:** Music-only, no video recording

**8Counts**
- Free on iOS
- Write out choreography in eight-count sheets
- Email sheets to team/students
- **Weakness:** Text-based only, no video/audio features

### 3. DANCE FITNESS / CASUAL
These are workout apps that use dance, not tools for dancers.

**DanceFitMe** — Fun dance workouts for women at home
**Just Dance Now** — Game-style dance-along app
**Lezhin Dance** — Dance workout content

None of these compete with CyphrCam. Different market entirely.

### 4. FORMATION / CHOREOGRAPHY PLANNING
**Choreographic** — Plan dance formations, animate transitions in 3D, sync to music
**ArrangeUs** — Formation visualization with music sync

These are for dance teams/directors, not individual dancers.

## RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Launch free, immediately.** DanceTribe is free. Dance.io is $6/mo. You can't charge before you have reviews.
2. **Add loop/speed/mirror ASAP** — these are table stakes. Without them, reviewers will compare unfavorably.

4. **Tell YOUR story** — none of these competitors are built by a dancer who actually uses the app. That's marketing gold.
5. **DanceTribe is the benchmark** — study their Instant Rec feature closely. Make sure Cyphr Sync is demonstrably better.
