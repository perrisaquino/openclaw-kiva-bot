# CyphrCam: Social vs. Private Experience Toggle
*Key Product Insight from Readwise Experience - Feb 26, 2026*

## The Learning

**From Perris's Readwise experience:**
> "I ghosted because I just didn't like the social aspects of it... it created kind of a performative reading environment with gamification being added to it."

## Product Insight for CyphrCam

**Core principle:** **User choice between social and private experiences**

### Implementation Strategy
- **Default:** Private experience (authentic creation without performance pressure)
- **Optional:** Social features can be toggled ON by users who want them
- **Never forced:** No requirement to engage socially to get full value

### Social Features (Optional Toggle)
**When enabled:**
- Share dance videos with community
- Like/comment on others' work
- Leaderboards or challenges
- Follow other dancers
- Public highlights or showcases

**When disabled:**
- Pure creation tool
- Private library/practice space
- No social pressure or comparison
- Focus on personal growth and expression

### Why This Matters for Dancers

**Movement is personal and vulnerable.** Many dancers:
- Want to practice without judgment
- Need space to experiment and fail
- Value authentic expression over performance
- Use dance for healing/personal growth

**But some dancers also want:**
- Community connection
- Inspiration from others
- Feedback and encouragement  
- Opportunities to showcase growth

## Design Philosophy

**"A studio you can bring anywhere"** - whether that's a private studio or a shared one.

**User autonomy over their creative experience** - let them choose the environment that serves their practice best.

## Implementation Notes

**Settings location:** 
- Privacy & Social tab in app settings
- Clear toggle: "Enable social features"
- Granular controls (share publicly vs. friends only, etc.)

**Default state:** Private (respects vulnerability of movement practice)

**Migration path:** Existing users get private by default, can opt into social

---

*This insight came from real user feedback (Perris's own experience) about how gamification and social features can interfere with authentic creative practice. Applying this to CyphrCam ensures we serve both the private practitioner and the social creator.*
