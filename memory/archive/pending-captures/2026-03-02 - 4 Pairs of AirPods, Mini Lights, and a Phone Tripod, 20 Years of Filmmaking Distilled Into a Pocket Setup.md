# 📝 4 Pairs of AirPods, Mini Lights, and a Phone Tripod, 20 Years of Filmmaking Distilled Into a Pocket Setup

**Date:** March 2, 2026 3:16 PM PST
**TL;DR:** Invested in 4 pairs of AirPods (different models, even got fakes by accident) to test Sound Disco mode. Constantly buying more minimal tripod solutions. The whole business runs off a phone, tripod, and AirPods. 20 years of filmmaking taught one lesson: the more friction in creating, the less you create. That minimalist conviction is the design DNA of CyphrCam.

## 🎯 Original Reflection
Like I invest a lot into building CyphrCam so I bought four pairs of AirPods in different models and even accidentally got some fake ones in order to test out the audio sharing feature, the sound disco mode, where people can dance with two pairs of headphones. You can have your own dual experience with a partner when you're dancing with them.

I'm also purchasing a lot of different types of phone tripods. I have one that's my favorite that I bring everywhere but I'm constantly investing in more and more minimal solutions so that if I go out and travel I can carry just a small bag and have everything I need to film a dance video or to practice a session anywhere. I go to a park with very little on me and I can run everything, even my whole entire business, off of my phone, a tripod, and AirPods. That's it; that's like I'm a really big minimalist even with OpenClaw and why I'm setting all this stuff up on my private server off of my Surface Book so that I don't have to bring a laptop anywhere. I can just do most things on my phone like I want to.

I know this is just a little bit of a thing about me: I just love minimal setups because I know that after being a filmmaker for 20 years, doing weddings, doing debuts, doing events, doing commercials, and doing short films on my own and with my own agency and stuff, the more friction there is in the process of creating something, the less likely you're gonna be able to do it. A lot of people think that the more expensive gear that they have, the better quality they're gonna get, the more viral they're gonna go, and all that bullshit, but that's, I think, one of the biggest limiting beliefs. I'm constantly trying to minimize my setup more. I used to have a big DSLR camera; I had a Canon 70D. I would use my dad's Canon 5D and then I got a Sony A7C with interchangeable lenses. While I still love that practice, I still think there's beauty to it when it comes to filming a dance video. I'm trying to push how far I can get with just using my phone.

I even have little mini lights; I have the Aputure MCs, the four pack, where I can just take those and be able to turn any environment at night into a solid lighting situation. I love very minimal camera gear; that's something that always geeks me out a lot. These are things: my minimal mindset is also a deep part of my design philosophy and why I like minimalism so that I'm focused on creating and not just getting the freaking tool to work. I just wanted to jot that down for another quick capture.
