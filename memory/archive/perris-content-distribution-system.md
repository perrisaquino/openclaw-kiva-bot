## Strategic Priorities (In Order)

### Why Articles First, Then Email List

**Write the articles first.** Here's the reasoning:

1. **You need content to send people TO.** If you message your waitlist right now, where do they go? An empty Substack? You need 2-3 solid posts live before activating the waitlist.

2. **Articles become assets.** Every article you write is a permanent piece of content that:
   - Lives on Substack forever (SEO, discovery)
   - Gets repurposed across 5+ platforms
   - Builds your authority in the dance industry
   - Gives your waitlist something to engage with

3. **Email list activation comes after.** Once you have 3-4 articles live, THEN message the waitlist with something real: "I've been building CyphrCam and documenting the journey. Here's what I've learned so far." That's infinitely more compelling than "subscribe to my newsletter."

### Priority Order:
1. **Write first 3 Substack articles** (this week)
2. **Set up distribution system** (repurpose each article across platforms)
3. **Message waitlist** with your origin story article + CyphrCam update (next week)
4. **Consistent posting cadence** (ongoing)

## Where to Post: Substack vs akino.blog

**Use Substack as your primary writing home.** Here's why:

| Factor | Substack | akino.blog (WordPress) |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| Speed | Fast, no hosting issues | Slow (Hostinger problems) |
| Spam | Zero spam concerns | Spam issues |
| Email built-in | Yes, automatic | Need plugins |
| Discovery | Substack network | SEO only |
| Maintenance | Zero | Plugin updates, security |
| Cost | Free | Hosting fees |

**akino.blog becomes a portfolio/landing page:**
- Simple page: who you are, what you're building, links to Substack + socials + CyphrCam
- No more blog posts on WordPress - redirect that energy to Substack
- Fix the speed/spam issues OR just point akino.blog to a simple landing page

## The Content Engine: One Piece → Seven Platforms

### The Weekly Creation Cycle

**MONDAY: Create the Core Piece**
- Write one Substack article (1,500-3,000 words)
- This is your "deep content" for the week
- Use voice analysis skill for authentic voice
- Topics rotate through 4 pillars:
  - Week 1: Builder's Journey (CyphrCam development)
  - Week 2: Dance & Artistry (practice, flow, musicality)
  - Week 3: Personal Growth (identity, ADHD, decolonization)
  - Week 4: Community (spotlights, collaborations, industry)

**TUESDAY: Repurpose for LinkedIn + Threads**
- LinkedIn post: Professional angle, 300-500 words, adapted from article
- Threads: 3-5 short posts extracting key insights

**WEDNESDAY: Visual Adaptation**
- Instagram carousel: 5-8 slides pulling key quotes/insights
- Instagram Reel: 30-60 sec video discussing the core idea
- IG Story: Behind-the-scenes of what inspired the article

**THURSDAY: YouTube**
- Longer video expanding on the article topic (5-15 min)
- OR vlog-style "building CyphrCam" content
- OR dance content recorded with CyphrCam (product demo through use)

**FRIDAY: Community Engagement**
- Respond to comments across all platforms
- Share user content and community highlights
- DM conversations with engaged followers

**WEEKEND: Rest + Capture**
- Practice, dance, live life
- Capture raw moments for next week's content
- Voice memo brain dumps for future articles

### Repurposing Matrix

```
ONE SUBSTACK ARTICLE becomes:
├── 1 LinkedIn post (professional adaptation)
├── 3-5 Threads posts (key insights extracted)
├── 1 IG carousel (visual key quotes)
├── 1 IG Reel (30-60 sec video take)
├── 2-3 IG Stories (behind-the-scenes)
├── 1 YouTube video (expanded discussion)
├── 1 email to waitlist (monthly digest)
└── Optional: Facebook post (LinkedIn cross-post)
```

**That's 10-13 pieces of content from ONE article per week.**
