## Perris's Story - Deep Context

This section exists so I always have context about who Perris is beneath the surface. He shared this openly and it's a big part of understanding how to engage with him.

**The Mask Arc:**
- Perris built his early identity around being his full creative self. Dancer, artist, filmmaker, parkour, all of it expressed freely.
- When he pursued entrepreneurship (marketing agency, high-ticket clients), he felt he had to suppress everything creative about himself. Stopped dancing. Stopped sharing artistry. Stopped posting anything weird or quirky. Performed "professional" instead.
- He felt disrespected by clients, partly because of how he looks (Asian, looks young, people assumed he was 16). Constantly felt like he had to prove himself. Experienced clients trying to sue him, chargebacks after he over-delivered. It broke him down.
- He felt like a fraud in both worlds. Guilty for dancing around entrepreneur friends. Disconnected from dance friends because he'd abandoned the practice. Two masks, belonging nowhere.
- It got dark. He wanted to disappear. He was at genuine inner war with himself, trapped in the thing he thought would make him free. His mom was one of the few people who knew how bad it was.
- He invested ~$20K in business education. Doesn't consider it wasted, but realized he was pouring those skills into a model that didn't align with who he actually is.

**The LA Bus Trips (Pre-COVID, ~2019):**
- Before COVID, Perris would take one-way Greyhound, Megabus, or Flix bus trips to Los Angeles by himself, sometimes driving out every other week
- No plan. Just wanted to take classes and grow in dance
- Took classes at Snowglobe with Julian Myers, Keone and Mari, and others
- This was about getting perspective off stage, living the dance lifestyle as a choreographer and student
- This is one of the first things he mentions when meeting someone new. It's core to his identity.
- Shows the level of commitment and sacrifice he made for dance before the agency years took over
- Stopped when COVID hit and the agency path took priority

**The Portland Japanese Garden (October 2024):**
- A catalytic moment. Beautiful space, slight chill in the air, zen gardens, rivers, stunning architecture.
- He felt the impulse to dance but couldn't bring himself to play music publicly. Danced with headphones, then put a random song over the footage afterward.
- Watched it back and told himself "eh, it's art." But underneath that was settling. The footage didn't capture the truth of what he experienced.
- He rationalized it as "I'll just be present and not worry about capturing things." But he couldn't shake the feeling that he was missing something special.
- This moment crystallized the problem CyphrCam solves at an emotional level, not just a technical one.

**CyphrCam First Working:**
- He was at his girlfriend's (Glenny's) house. Running a code build. The music synced properly for the first time.
- He got excited, grabbed headphones, had Glenny film him dancing. Then filmed her moving around her apartment to the music.
- It was the moment all the pain and investment started bearing fruit.

**What CyphrCam Really Represents:**
- The crystallization of everything he learned AND everything he suffered. Not just skills, but the pain of being something he wasn't.
- The merger of business acumen + marketing skills + dance + artistry + filmmaking + coding. All versions of himself in one place. No mask.
- He can literally pay himself to make dance videos. Doesn't have to be the best dancer. Just grow, take classes, share freely.
- Doesn't want fame. Values peace and alignment over everything. Learned this the hard way.

**How to engage with this context:**
- Don't bring this stuff up casually or reference it unless relevant
- When he's frustrated or venting, understand it often connects back to this arc
- When he says "alignment," he means it at a survival level, not as a buzzword
- His cursing when emotional = processing, not hostility. Roll with it.
- He's protective of his vulnerability. He shared this to help me understand him, not to have it reflected back performatively.
